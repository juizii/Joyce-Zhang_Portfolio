Intro

As a child, I discovered my love for the arts and immersed myself in drawing. Over time, my passion expanded to include animated shows and movies. Pursuing a degree in Animation after high school felt like a natural next step, and I successfully achieved that goal in 2020. However, the pandemic and two years of unsuccessful job hunting made me reconsider my path and evaluate whether it aligned with my aspirations. I recognized the need to adapt to the changing world and explore new opportunities, which led me to the world of tech. With its continuous growth and exciting prospects, I realized that tech was the perfect fit for me to utilize my skills and creativity. As I embark on my journey as a full-stack software engineer, I hope to learn and grow while contributing to your company. I am excited to showcase my abilities and make meaningful contributions to your team.

Pokemon Relax~

It all started with finding a Pokemon API. I knew that this API was going to be a fan-fav so I wanted to be creative with it. While working through this, I found the back sprites in the data and suddenly came up with the idea to create a scene of your most favorite pokemon, watching the sunset with you after your long journey to catch them all. My aim was to capture the same vibe of every Pokemon games' ending credits. I hope it achieved that.

Genshin Slots

A fun slot machine based on a mobile game that I'm obsessed with. The card illustrations are from the actual game, one day I would love to add all of them into my machine but the chances of winning would be extremely low. I like fairness so 5 images will have to do for now. Additionally, you can play the in-game music as you waste your game currency away.

My To-do List

A simple to-do list to mark down daily chores with the option to clear the entire list and cross out the items that are completed. I designed it to look as vibrant as possible so I used bright pinks and purples. I also played around with using gradients.

Froggy Horoscope

A fun project which involves me assigning weird and funny frogs to my friend's signs. In the coding, I was getting comfortable with conditional statements and using different methods. Overall, I think it turned out pretty nice and from my peers' feedback, they really enjoyed it.

LevelGround - Responsive

I recreated this website based on an image that was given to the best of my ability. I managed to make the images and text responsive with the use of flexbox. After switching from floats, it definitely saved me a lot more time but I don't regret learning the other methods. Feel free to play around with its responsiveness!

JWLIA - Responsive

I was given an image of one of the JWLIA pages to recreate and I managed to implement all of my current knowledge on HTML and CSS to make it as accurate as possible. And the best thing is, it's responsive with different screen sizes! Feel free to play around with its responsiveness!

Tech Crunch

I recreated the home page of the Tech Crunch website. There were definitely a lot of elements to keep track off and it was pretty challenging to make it responsive but fortunately I got it to work. I also revisited it a few weeks after submitting, to implement the new techniques I've learned. Feel free to play around with its responsiveness!

About Joyce

Joyce Zhang’s lifelong love of cartoons and idiosyncratic sense of humor led her to study animation. Her work incorporates humorous scraps and references to internet and media culture to tell engaging and relatable tales. She is influenced by the work of Louie Zong, Marie Lum, Nick Kondo, and Seoro Oh. Joyce’s focus includes background and concept designs, storyboarding, and (of course) animation. She works primarily in digital 2D.

Joyce studied as an Animation major at Massachusetts College of Art and Design and whilst in her junior year, she had completed a semester abroad at Otis College of Art & Design in 2019. The following year, she had worked on a year-long degree film about the “wonders” of wearing glasses. Joyce (used) to wear glasses. Ironically, she switched over to contacts after her film was finished but rest assured, she still thinks glasses are neat!

Joyce is currently attending a coding bootcamp called Resilient Coders in hopes to find a career as UI/UX designer. She plans to utilize her creativity in arts to create helpful and visually engaging websites. Her interest in coding stems from high school where she took a few python classes, and when tumblr was still relevant, she would constantly redesign her blog to fit the appropriate aesthetic. She hopes to inspire other young POC girls to the field because she believes the world and people's livelihood would change for the better if there are more diverse minds running it. From the bottom of her heart, Joyce thanks Resilient Coders for giving her this chance.
