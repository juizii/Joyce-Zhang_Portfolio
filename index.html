Intro

As a child, I discovered my love for the arts and immersed myself in drawing. Over time, my passion expanded to include animated shows and movies. Pursuing a degree in Animation after high school felt like a natural next step, and I successfully achieved that goal in 2020. However, the pandemic made me reconsider my path and evaluate whether it aligned with my aspirations. I recognized the need to adapt to the changing world and explore new opportunities, which led me to the world of tech. With its continuous growth and exciting prospects, I realized that tech was the perfect fit for me to utilize my skills and creativity. As I embark on my journey as a full-stack software engineer, I hope to learn and grow while contributing to your company. I will always value growth and be a lifelong learner, even within my software engineering career. I am excited to showcase my abilities and make meaningful contributions to your team.

About Joyce

Joyce Zhang’s lifelong love of cartoons and idiosyncratic sense of humor led her to study animation. Her work incorporates humorous scraps and references to internet and media culture to tell engaging and relatable tales. She is influenced by the work of Louie Zong, Marie Lum, Nick Kondo, and Seoro Oh. Joyce’s focus includes background and concept designs, storyboarding, and (of course) animation. She works primarily in digital 2D.

Joyce studied as an Animation major at Massachusetts College of Art and Design and whilst in her junior year, she had completed a semester abroad at Otis College of Art & Design in 2019. The following year, she had worked on a year-long degree film about the “wonders” of wearing glasses. Joyce (used) to wear glasses. Ironically, she switched over to contacts after her film was finished but rest assured, she still thinks glasses are neat!

Joyce attended a coding bootcamp called Resilient Coders in hopes to find a career as UI/UX designer. She plans to utilize her creativity in arts to create helpful and visually engaging websites. Her interest in coding stems from high school where she took a few python classes, and when tumblr was still relevant, she would constantly redesign her blog to fit the appropriate aesthetic. She hopes to inspire other young POC girls to the field because she believes the world and people's livelihood would change for the better if there are more diverse minds running it.
